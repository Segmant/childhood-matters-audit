Section 1 — Legal Compliance

Three legal requirements
are currently unmet

  • These are not optional improvements — they are legal requirements
  • Until resolved, the business is exposed to regulatory risk under UK law
  • The ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) actively investigates and fines for exactly these breaches
  • I recommend addressing all three of these before anything else on this list
Critical

No cookie consent banner — breach of UK GDPR and PECR

  • No cookie notice appears when a visitor lands on the site — cookies are set without knowledge or consent
  • UK GDPR — visitors must be informed before any personal data is processed
  • PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations) — explicit opt-in consent is required before any non-essential cookies are placed on a visitor's device
  • This is a direct breach of both and must be the first thing fixed
https://childhoodmatters.co.uk
No cookie banner is present. The page loads in full with no consent notice, no opt-in, and no opt-out. A compliant site would display a consent layer before any tracking activates.
  • MonsterInsights (Google Analytics plugin) and WP Statistics are both installed — both can set tracking cookies
  • Neither is currently gated behind any form of consent
  • The following comment is visible in the site's source code, visible to anyone who looks:
<!-- This site uses the Google Analytics by MonsterInsights plugin v10.2.2 -->
<!-- Note: MonsterInsights is not currently configured on this site. -->
<!-- The site owner needs to authenticate with Google Analytics -->
<!-- in the MonsterInsights settings panel. -->
<!-- No tracking code set -->
What this means
  • The plugin is live and publicly advertising that GA is not set up
  • When GA is connected, it will immediately track visitors without consent unless the banner is in place first

Recommendation

  • Install CookieYes or Complianz (both have free tiers designed for UK compliance)
  • Configure the banner to block all non-essential scripts until consent is given
  • Set WP Statistics to anonymise IPs and disable its own cookie in plugin settings
  • Only connect Google Analytics after the consent banner is in place and tested
Critical

No Privacy Policy published

  • UK GDPR requires any organisation collecting personal data to publish a Privacy Policy
  • The contact form collects names, email addresses, phone numbers, and organisation details — this alone makes a Privacy Policy mandatory
  • There is currently no Privacy Policy page anywhere on the site
https://childhoodmatters.co.uk/contact/
Contact page — personal data is collected here with no reference to a Privacy Policy. A name, telephone number, organisation, and email address are requested with no explanation of how this data is handled.

Recommendation

  • Write and publish a Privacy Policy at /privacy-policy/
  • Include a link to it on the contact form ("By submitting this form, you agree to our Privacy Policy")
  • Add a link to the footer so it is accessible from every page
Critical

No Cookie Policy published

  • PECR requires a Cookie Policy listing every cookie set, its purpose, and its lifespan
  • This does not exist on the site
  • Most cookie consent plugins generate this automatically — so it is largely resolved as a by-product of fixing the consent banner

Recommendation

  • Allow the cookie consent plugin (CookieYes or Complianz) to auto-generate a cookie policy page
  • Review and customise the generated content to ensure accuracy
  • Link the cookie policy from the footer and from within the consent banner

Section 2 — Analytics

The business is flying blind
— no visitor data is being captured

  • Right now there is no way to know how many people visit, where they come from, or whether any visitor has ever made an enquiry as a result of the site
  • This must be resolved — but only after the cookie consent banner is in place
Important

Google Analytics is installed but not connected

  • MonsterInsights is installed but has never been authenticated — no GA data has ever been collected
  • WP Statistics is active but only provides basic page view counts
  • No traffic source data, no user behaviour, no conversion tracking, no demographic information
  • No link to Google Search Console — no visibility into which search terms bring people to the site

Recommendation

  • Create or connect a Google Analytics 4 (GA4) property at analytics.google.com
  • Authenticate MonsterInsights with the GA4 account via the plugin settings panel
  • Ensure the cookie consent plugin gates GA — it must only fire for visitors who have given consent
  • Connect Google Search Console and link it to the GA4 property for keyword-level data
  • Set up a monthly review of GA4 data to inform content and marketing decisions

Section 3 — Search Engine Optimisation

Straightforward fixes that will
improve Google visibility

  • Yoast SEO is installed and the sitemap is correctly configured — good foundations
  • However, several important on-page elements have not been set up
  • These are straightforward fixes that will directly improve how the site performs in Google
Important

Homepage title is not optimised

  • Current title: "Home - Childhood Matters" — tells Google nothing about what the business does
  • It will not rank for any relevant search term
  • Suggested title: Educational Consultancy & SEND Support | Childhood Matters
  • Every page on the site needs a unique, keyword-led title — currently none of them have one

Recommendation

  • Update the homepage title in Yoast SEO to include primary keywords
  • Write unique, keyword-informed titles for all 24 pages — none should read "Page Name - Site Name" without context
  • Titles should be 50–60 characters and lead with the most important keyword
Important

Meta descriptions not set on any pages

  • Meta descriptions are the text that appears under each result in Google — they directly influence whether someone clicks your result or a competitor's
  • None of the 24 pages have a meta description set in Yoast
  • Google is falling back to the first line of page content, which rarely reads well in a search context

Recommendation

  • Write a unique meta description for each of the 24 pages
  • Keep them to 150–160 characters
  • Include a clear value proposition and a soft call to action where appropriate
Important

Five consultant pages have non-descriptive URLs

  • Five consultant pages were duplicated from a template without updating the URL slug
  • Two pages are correctly named — /associate-consultant-danielle-ford/ and /associate-consultant-victoria-white/ — all pages should follow this pattern
  • Generic numeric URLs provide no SEO value and look unprofessional if shared
childhoodmatters.co.uk/associate-consultant-2/
childhoodmatters.co.uk/associate-consultant-3/
childhoodmatters.co.uk/associate-consultant-4/
childhoodmatters.co.uk/associate-consultant-5/
childhoodmatters.co.uk/associate-consultant-6/

Recommendation

  • Update each URL slug to use the consultant's name (e.g. /associate-consultant-dr-martin-bloomfield/)
  • Set up automatic 301 redirects from the old numeric URLs to avoid broken links
Improve

No Google Business Profile or local SEO signals

  • No physical or registered address appears anywhere on the site
  • No Google Business Profile appears to be set up
  • Important for trust and for appearing in regional or location-based searches
  • Google Reviews via a Business Profile are one of the highest-trust signals available — and they're free

Recommendation

  • Add a registered business address to the footer and contact page
  • Create and verify a Google Business Profile at business.google.com
  • Enable Google Reviews via the Business Profile — these appear directly in search results

Section 4 — Content & Conversion

The site informs,
but doesn't yet convert

  • The site describes services well but is not yet working hard enough as a business development tool
  • Several content gaps are reducing trust and making it harder for visitors to take the next step
Important

Testimonials are thin and anonymous

  • Only four testimonials on the site — all anonymous, with no name, school, or organisation attached
  • Attributed only to generic descriptors such as "Head of Secondary, Community Special School"
  • Headteachers and trust leaders commissioning consultancy at this level expect verifiable references
  • Anonymous quotes carry very little weight with this audience
https://childhoodmatters.co.uk/testimonials/
Testimonials page — four anonymous quotes with no names, organisations, or dates. Decision-makers at headteacher and trust level expect verifiable references, not unattributed quotes.

Recommendation

  • Seek permission from satisfied clients to use their name and organisation name
  • Build towards a minimum of 10–15 testimonials
  • Set up Google Reviews for third-party verified social proof that appears in search results
  • Distribute testimonials across relevant service pages, not just on a single standalone page
Opportunity

No case studies — the most persuasive content type is missing

  • No case studies exist anywhere on the site
  • For a consultancy, case studies are consistently the highest-converting content type
  • A short account of the challenge, the approach, and the outcome — improved attendance, a positive Ofsted, a child back in education — is far more persuasive than a service description alone
  • Schools or families who prefer anonymity can be presented without names

Recommendation

  • Develop two to three case studies to begin with
  • Schools or families who prefer anonymity can be presented without names (e.g. "A primary school in the East Midlands")
  • Publish case studies as standalone pages and link them from the relevant service sections
Improve

No pricing information on service pages

  • Named packages exist for attendance, inclusion/SEND, and safeguarding — but none include any indication of cost
  • Some visitors will leave rather than make an enquiry just to find out if the service is within budget
  • Even a starting-from price or day rate range removes that barrier
https://childhoodmatters.co.uk/educational-consultancy-services/
Services page — packages are listed with no indication of cost. Even a starting-from price or day rate range would help visitors self-qualify and reduce the friction of making an initial enquiry.

Recommendation

  • Add indicative pricing, day rate ranges, or package tier descriptions to service pages
  • At minimum, add a statement such as "Packages are tailored to your requirements — contact us for a no-obligation quote"
  • Make it easy for visitors to understand what happens after they enquire
Improve

Contact page is missing key information

  • Phone number and email are present — good
  • No physical or registered business address
  • No response time commitment — visitors don't know what to expect after submitting the form
  • No indication of next steps — these small details reduce uncertainty and increase conversions

Recommendation

  • Add a registered business address
  • Add a response time commitment ("We aim to respond within one working day")
  • Add a brief note on next steps after the form is submitted
  • Add a LinkedIn link to the company page as an additional contact route

Section 7 — My Standard Working Practices

How I build every site
I take on

  • Every site I build is delivered on the same proven technical foundation
  • These aren't optional extras — they are standard on every project and make a tangible difference to speed, security, stability, and long-term value
  • Below is exactly what I would implement for Childhood Matters from day one
1

Your site added to my Cloudflare account for speed and security

  • Global CDN (Content Delivery Network) — your site's assets are served from data centres worldwide, meaning visitors load the site from a location physically close to them rather than from a single server In plain English: your website loads quickly for everyone, wherever they are — not just people near the server
  • DDoS protection — Cloudflare sits in front of your server and absorbs malicious traffic at scale, protecting the site from distributed attacks that could otherwise take it offline entirely In plain English: if someone tries to flood your website with fake traffic to knock it offline, Cloudflare stops it before it ever reaches you
  • Web Application Firewall (WAF) — intelligent rules that block malicious bots, scrapers, and known attack patterns before they ever reach your site or server In plain English: a security filter that blocks hackers and automated attacks the moment they appear — before they can do any damage
  • Free SSL/TLS certificate — end-to-end encryption between visitor and server, automatically renewed, with HTTP automatically upgraded to HTTPS In plain English: this is the padlock you see in the browser address bar — it means every visitor's connection to your site is private and secure
  • Caching at the edge — static assets and pages are cached at Cloudflare's network level, dramatically reducing server load and improving response times even during traffic spikes In plain English: your most visited pages are stored and ready to go, so they load almost instantly instead of being built fresh every time someone visits
  • Bot management — automated filtering of bad bots that inflate analytics figures, scrape content, or probe for vulnerabilities In plain English: filters out fake automated visitors so your analytics only show real people, and stops programs scanning the site for weaknesses
  • Always-on protection — even if the origin server has a brief issue, Cloudflare can serve a cached version of the site to visitors so it remains accessible In plain English: if there's ever a brief problem with the server, your website stays online for visitors — they won't see an error page
  • Real-time analytics — Cloudflare provides an additional layer of traffic analytics, including blocked threats and bandwidth saved, at no cost In plain English: a live dashboard showing how much traffic your site receives, how many threats were blocked, and how fast it's performing — all included for free
2

Built on Oxygen Builder with WordPress — not Elementor

  • Significantly faster load times — Elementor loads a large amount of its own code on every page regardless of what is actually used. Oxygen generates only the code each page needs, resulting in leaner, faster pages that score better on Google PageSpeed and Core Web Vitals In plain English: pages built in Oxygen load noticeably faster than the same pages built in Elementor — because there's no unnecessary code running in the background
  • Cleaner HTML output — Elementor wraps every element in multiple layers of unnecessary code with inline styles. Oxygen produces clean, minimal markup that is easier for Google to read and index In plain English: the underlying code is neat and tidy — Google finds it easier to understand what your page is about, which helps your rankings
  • Better SEO by default — Google uses page speed and Core Web Vitals as ranking signals. A site built in Oxygen consistently outperforms the same design built in Elementor on these tests, which translates directly into better search visibility In plain English: Google rewards fast, well-coded websites with better rankings — Oxygen gives your site a head start over competitors still using Elementor
  • No reliance on a third-party plugin for layout — Elementor is a plugin that must be kept active at all times. If it breaks or conflicts with a WordPress update, every page on the site is affected. Oxygen compiles its output directly into the site, making it far more robust In plain English: your site's layout doesn't depend on a third-party company's software staying active and up to date — it's built into the site itself
  • Full design control — Oxygen gives complete pixel-level control over every element with no pre-built widget constraints. The design is built to your exact specification, not fitted around a template In plain English: everything looks exactly as designed — not "close enough" because a drag-and-drop widget wouldn't quite do what was needed
  • No shortcode lock-in — Elementor embeds shortcodes throughout page content. If the plugin is ever removed or deactivated, those shortcodes appear as broken text and pages fall apart. Oxygen does not do this In plain English: your content is never held hostage by the page builder — if anything ever changes, your text and content remain completely intact
  • Reduced plugin overhead — many things Elementor requires additional plugins to achieve are built natively into Oxygen, resulting in fewer active plugins and a more stable, faster WordPress installation In plain English: fewer plugins running means fewer things that can go wrong, break after an update, or slow the site down
  • Built for developers, not just designers — Oxygen supports custom code at a component level, making it far more powerful for building bespoke, functional sites without workarounds In plain English: if you ever need something bespoke — a custom form, a special layout, a unique feature — it can be built properly rather than patched together
3

Hosted on my own managed VPS server

  • Dedicated resources — on shared hosting, your site shares a server with hundreds of others. A spike in traffic on a neighbouring site can slow yours down. On a VPS, your site has its own allocated CPU and RAM that no one else can consume In plain English: your website has its own private space on the server — what other websites do cannot affect yours
  • Full server control — I configure the server specifically for WordPress: PHP version, memory limits, caching, database optimisation, and redirects are all set to best-practice standards rather than generic defaults In plain English: every setting on the server is tuned for your site specifically — not left on the out-of-the-box defaults that most hosting companies use
  • Optimised server stack — the server runs nginx, PHP 8.x, and Redis object caching — a combination that delivers significantly better performance than standard managed WordPress hosting In plain English: the software powering the server is chosen and configured for speed — the same way a performance car needs the right engine, not just any engine
  • Enhanced security — the server is hardened at the operating system level: firewall rules, brute-force protection, key-only login access, regular security patching, and automatic malware scanning In plain English: the server is locked down at every level — not just the website itself, but the machine it runs on
  • Automated daily backups — full site and database backups are taken automatically every day and stored offsite. The site can be restored to any previous point quickly and without data loss In plain English: a complete copy of your website is saved every single day — if anything ever goes wrong, nothing is lost and it can be restored quickly
  • Staging environment — a private copy of the live site can be maintained on the same server, allowing updates, design changes, and new features to be fully tested before going live In plain English: changes are tested on a hidden copy of the site first — visitors never see anything half-finished or broken
  • No arbitrary resource caps — generic hosting plans impose artificial limits on traffic, storage, or database queries. On the VPS, limits are defined by the actual server specification, not a plan tier In plain English: there are no surprise "you've hit your limit" messages — the site can grow without being held back by an arbitrary hosting plan
  • Single point of contact — hosting, performance, security, and the website itself are all managed by me. There is no confusion about who to call when something needs attention In plain English: one person handles everything — no being passed between a hosting company and a web developer while a problem sits unresolved
  • Cost efficiency at scale — as more sites are hosted on the same VPS, the per-site hosting cost reduces, making it a more economical long-term arrangement than separate managed hosting plans In plain English: hosting on my server is better value than paying separately for a managed hosting plan — and the quality of service is higher
